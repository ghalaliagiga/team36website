After achieving a basic understanding of the functionalities of SketchUP and AutoCAD, we embarked on a journey towards implementing speech navigation in these softwares. To accomplish this, we needed to update our speech recognition system so that its vocabulary could be tailored to the specific software being used. This allowed for the reliable triggering of speech commands, particularly for difficult words such as "orbit" which often got misinterpreted as "or bit".

Our approach involved passing a JSON list of acceptable words to the KaldiRecognizer object, which enabled us to narrow down the range of words that the system could recognize and respond to. With this enhancement in place, we proceeded to test the efficiency of the system on the CAD softwares, which turned out to be successful.

Having established the reliability and effectiveness of our speech navigation system, we decided to create a demo video to showcase how users can easily switch between the features of each CAD software using speech commands.

Following our successful implementation of speech commands in SketchUP and AutoCAD, we decided to expand our testing to other CAD software. One of the software packages we chose to focus on was TinkerCAD, which is known for its ease of use and interactive interface.

Our decision to test MotionInput with TinkerCAD was motivated by the fact that we were scheduled to present our work to a board who were particularly interested in the potential for MotionInput to enhance child play experiences. We recognized the need to demonstrate that our system could function seamlessly with user-friendly software such as TinkerCAD, which is a popular choice among children and beginners.

Our testing revealed that MotionInput worked effectively in navigating TinkerCAD using speech commands. We were able to move through the software's features with ease and our demonstration went smoothly, with little to no problems encountered during the testing process.
