Critical Evaluation

User Interface

For our project, we took the user interface as a top priority and invested a significant amount of time in iterating and designing the best possible design. Our team has designed the user interface and system with a high level of intuitiveness and ease of understanding. We have carefully considered every aspect of the design, from the layout to the color scheme, to ensure that it is visually appealing and easy to use. By prioritizing the user interface design, we have created a system that is both intuitive and user-friendly, allowing users to fully immerse themselves in the experience. The design has also made it easier for users to learn and understand the system, reducing the need for training or support. 5 / 5


Functionality

In terms of the requirements given to us and the goals we created, our project has completed all of these and even surpassed. The MoSCoW list is essentially all completed ignoring the requirements concerning PseudoVR but this is because we changed project direction. Throughout the project, although we faced some unexpected obstacles, we maintained excellent communication and worked together to create new and creative solutions. 4 / 5


Stability

Testing was important to us so that we could ensure our program was up to a good enough standard to be used and so that it surpassed all the given requirements. Given the range of testing that was conducted, from unit testing to user acceptance testing, we can instil confidence in the stability of our program and its usability amongst users. 5 / 5


Efficiency

The program includes smooth dragging for most gesture modes but very rarely faces some minor problems for example in recognizing speech commands. This has already been mostly combatted with specific dictionaries and filtering of words that are irrelevant. Fortunately, these do not greatly affect the overall efficiency of the program. 4 / 5


Compatability

Our team has conducted thorough testing to ensure that our program is compatible with a wide range of Windows devices that have the necessary hardware. This includes a working microphone to detect speech commands and a webcam for facial and hand gestures. However, due to its specific development for the Windows platform, it cannot be used on devices that run on alternate operating systems. . 3 / 5


Maintainability

As we collaborated with several teams on the project, we prioritized the organization and maintainability of our code. Our aim was to ensure that future teams could effortlessly build upon our work and extend the project to meet evolving needs.


To achieve this goal, we employed industry best practices in software development, including modular design, clear documentation, and efficient coding techniques. Our team recognized that maintainable code is critical for long-term success, and we implemented robust testing and debugging processes to detect and resolve issues quickly. We focused on creating a scalable and flexible architecture that could accommodate future updates and enhancements without major code overhauls. This approach allowed us to reduce technical debt and minimize the risk of project stagnation. 5 / 5


Project Management

Our team was highly organized and maintained excellent communication throughout the project. We held regular meetings, typically on a weekly basis, and made sure to take detailed meeting notes for reference and organization. This approach allowed us to closely monitor our progress and make informed decisions for the future of the project.


Thanks to our well-structured approach, we were able to face obstacles with relative ease. Even when faced with challenges, our team was able to remain resilient and work together to overcome them. In essence, our organized approach had a positive impact on the project, enabling us to operate smoothly and efficiently even in the face of unexpected hurdles. 5 / 5

Known Bugs

It's essential to evaluate a project's successes and areas of improvement continually. While we are proud of the progress we have made, there are still some bugs and challenges we faced. One area that requires further improvement is the reliability of facial navigation and dragging. Specifically, we noticed that users experience lag and inaccuracies when attempting to move to the right. This issue is particularly problematic when trying to navigate a 3D space, where precision is key. Another issue we faced was related to our facial gestures mode. While it was designed to work seamlessly with speech commands, we noticed that some users accidentally triggered a smiling gesture when speaking. This problem is especially concerning as it can lead to conflicting or unwanted commands being executed. However, by identifying these issues, we can continue to refine and improve our system, ensuring that it meets the needs and expectations of our users.

Future work


  • Looking ahead, an area for improvement in our project is the usability of facial navigation. To enhance the user experience, it is important to implement changes that will make this feature function more efficiently and intuitively.
  • We aim to enhance the usability of MotionInput by extending its compatibility to a wider range of CAD software. This will allow it to become much more widley used across a range of ages and professions.
