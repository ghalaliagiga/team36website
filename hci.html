HCI

Direct Observation

Observation Sample:

1. The architect walked up to the display. After they waved to start the demo, they raised their left hand to locate the cursor and click the 'Enter house' button.
2. They then hesitated to decide which room to visit from the floor plan that popped up next.
3. They nodded their head as they clicked the first room – kitchen. They stopped and looked slightly puzzled as they tilt their head. They turned to the interviewer and asked if they can look around the virtual room.
4. After the interviewer explained the pinch gesture for panning the camera, the architect smiles and successfully started looking around the room.
5. The client stepped in and had a look themselves.

1-to-1 semi-structured interviews

How did you feel upon opening the display?

I felt hesitant to use gestures at first, but the instructions were clear and the navigation by pinching buttons was surprisingly easy to use.
Did you feel that the main menu and instructions were easy to understand? I felt that everything was labelled clearly and it was obvious to me which buttons I had to press to access different parts of the navigation, however I wouldn't have thought to pan the camera with my hands.
Were you able to present the property in a satisfactory way to your client? Yes, the floor plan menu allowed me to easily access which room I wanted, and the client enjoyed being able to cycle through the rooms already visited.
How does this compare how you would perform this task without the software? I found the application a big benefit for presentations, I can see myself integrating this into my day-to-day work providing the setup isn't too much of a hassle.


Personas

Glenda (architect)

Glenda is an experienced architect who has been presenting her properties to clients for many decades but has little experience with using technology in her work.  On an average day, Glenda helps her clients view her properties and look around to view different sections or rooms. She wants to learn how to use this software to hopefully improve her position at work and increase her client's satisfaction. She is currently in a wheelchair due to her recent hip-replacement surgery and therefore  requires accessibility adjustments.
Upon opening our app, Glenda took a systematic approach, taking time to read the main menu and appreciating that the program contained options to input data whilst seated instead of standing. Glenda was happy that she could enter each room based on the floorplan; a functionality of the app that mapped very well her daily life. She also specifically liked that, as the architect, she could sit down with her clients and explain all parts of the property in contrast to before where she wasn’t as involved in the properties viewing due to her disability.



Bryan (undergraduate student)

Bryan is currently an interior design student who has basic subject knowledge and has experience using technology in his daily life. Whilst at university, Bryan already utilizes similar programs and he believes that learning how to this application will be a beneficial addition to his skillset. Furthermore, it would also increase his chances in securing an internship considering his burgeoning interest in using CAD software. 
When Bryan opened our app, he dove straight into working out how to utilize its features via trial and error. Due to his technology and gaming background, he quickly became very familiar with the environment. He quickly was able to understand the options in the main menu and was soon enjoying navigating around the house whilst  using the orbiting features to look in detail around each room.  It was easy for him to learn how to navigate throughout the house and he didn’t waste much time adapting to the new environment.
