Project background

MotionInput is a technology that enables touchless interaction with a computer, making it possible for people with disabilities to use technology in a way that was previously impossible. However, while MotionInput can facilitate general traversal, it falls short in more complex situations. Our goal is to make 3D design tools accessible to everyone, especially children with limited mobility, by extending MotionInput's capabilities to work with CAD software. By doing so, we can empower individuals who may have been previously excluded from this field to unleash their creativity and bring their ideas to life.

Project goals

The aim of our project is to expand the functionality of MotionInput, allowing it to be used seamlessly within various CAD software programs, including Tinkercad, Autocad, and Sketchup. By integrating MotionInput with these programs, we can empower users to intuitively navigate through the software using hand, speech, and facial tracking features. Our program will offer a range of capabilities and features that are specifically tailored to architectural design programs, providing users with the tools they need to create complex 3D designs with ease.

With this capability, users can easily manipulate objects, create sketches, and perform a range of other tasks with simple gestures, facial expressions, or vocal commands. This approach not only improves the user experience but also makes the software more accessible for individuals with limited mobility or other disabilities that may affect their ability to use traditional input methods.

Requirement gathering

Following a thorough investigation into the background of our project, we decided to initiate the process of gathering requirements. In order to achieve this, we employed a direct observation in the field technique, which was followed by a series of 1-to-1 semi-structured interviews with the architect/designer. This approach allowed us to engage in extensive discussions regarding the features that our client deemed a priority, and address any obstacles that were present within the workplace.

As a result of this approach, we were able to receive detailed requirements that were tailored to the specific needs of our client. These included:


  • The implementation of speech commands and shortcuts to enhance usability
  • The ability to manipulate objects in the environment with ease
  • Facial tracking for enhanced user experience
  • Smooth dragging gestures for Sketchup and Autocad
  • The creation of MFC settings that were tailored to hand, face, and speech tracking.

By taking the time to gather and carefully consider these requirements, we were able to develop a solution that met the unique needs and preferences of our client, and helped them to achieve their desired outcomes.
