MotionInput

To start our project, we had to conduct a thorough investigation of MotionInput's existing technologies and applications. Our research revealed that the software was developed by academics and students at UCL, providing users with the ability to interact with their computer through touchless technology. MotionInput uses a webcam to detect various gestures made by the user's face, head, hands, full body, and speech. These interactions are analyzed by the software, which converts them into signals, allowing the user to control their device effortlessly.

To ensure that our project was an improvement over the previous version, we conducted a comprehensive analysis of the codebase. We carefully scrutinized the design and functionality of the software, identifying any issues that could be addressed to enhance its performance. This process enabled us to compile a comprehensive list of requirements for our project.


To achieve our goals, we began by examining the current source code repository and testing it on various devices. This allowed us to familiarize ourselves with the software's operation and gain an understanding of how it functions. We carefully analyzed the code, looking for areas that required improvement or optimization to enhance the software's functionality. Through this process, we identified several key areas that could be improved, and we developed strategies to address these issues in our project. Our research formed the foundation for the development of a robust and efficient software system that would meet the needs of our clients and users.



CAD softwares

Our project had a specific focus on customizing MotionInput for CAD software. To achieve this goal, we dedicated time to researching various CAD software available in the market. CAD, or computer-aided design, software is a valuable tool that enables users to digitally create 2D drawings and 3D models of real-world products before they are manufactured. In our research, we explored the software features and functionality of prominent CAD software such as AutoCAD and SketchUp.


AutoCAD, a CAD software developed by AutoDesk, is primarily designed for architectural design. Its interface is sophisticated and complex, but it has a broad range of features that make creating industrial-specific designs more straightforward. With its ability to work in 2D and 3D planes, the software is highly versatile, catering to different professionals' needs. We delved into the software's various modes and features, creating an extensive list for reference.


SketchUp is another CAD software that is well-suited for 3D modelling purposes, including architectural design. Compared to AutoCAD, SketchUp has a simpler interface, with fewer functions and a more interactive workspace. Like AutoCAD, it also operates in both 2D and 3D planes, which further expands its potential applications.


While AutoCAD and SketchUp were both great choices for our project's focus on architectural design, we also researched the usage of TinkerCAD. As we progressed with our project, we realized that including a software that was simpler to use and more suitable for all ages would be more intuitive. TinkerCAD is an ideal choice as it can be used for creating architectural designs and modelling objects, as well as programming blocks. With its versatility and user-friendly interface, TinkerCAD is well-suited for individuals of all ages and skill levels.
